Clear space We maintain a clear space around the logo to preserve its integrity and separate it from competing graphic elements. A minimum clear space equal to half the height of the logo should be applied around it. Negative logo Positive logo Placement on backgrounds When the logo needs to be placed on a coloured background or over a photo, you need to make sure the logo shows up clearly. Where possible, always choose background colours or photos that give a good contrast with our logo. White backgrounds The logo should be used with the red rectangle. Red and black backgrounds The corporate logo rectangle blends in with a Pantone 032 or black background when we use a red Pantone or mono logo.
The borders of the original background must not be changed or cropped. Colour and photographic backgrounds The logo should be used with the red rectangle, so in exactly the same way as we would on a white background. Dual branding Sometimes, we’ll need to display our brand alongside other brands. When we do this, we need to make sure that our logo is displayed in the right way, so we don’t lose any of our visibility as a brand. Here’s what to do in different scenarios. In vertical applications Logos from other brands should be positioned above our logo, so ours is at the bottom. The minimum distance between the top of our logo and the bottom of the logo above should be half the height of our logo. In horizontal applications Logos from other brands should be positioned to the left of our logo. The minimum space between the other brand’s logo and our logo should be the same as the height of our logo.
