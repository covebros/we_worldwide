Clear space We maintain a clear space around the logo to preserve its integrity and separate it from competing graphic elements. A minimum clear space equal to half the height of the logo should be applied. Negative logo Positive logo Placement on backgrounds With a logo placed on a coloured background or photo, the logo should be clear and visible. Where possible, always choose background colours or photos that provide a good contrast against the logo. White backgrounds Use the negative logo. Red and black backgrounds The negative logo blends with Pantone 032 and the black logo blends with a black background.
The borders of the original logo must not be changed or cropped. Colour and photographic backgrounds The logo should be used with the red rectangle the same way as on a white background. Dual branding Sometimes, we'll need to display our brand alongside others. When this happens we want to ensure we don't lose any of our visibility as a brand. Here's what to do in different scenarios. In vertical applications Logos from other brands should be positioned above our logo. The space between the two logos should be half the height of our logo. In horizontal applications Logos from other brands should be positioned to the left of our logo. The space between the two logos should be the height of our logo.
